Great question! The provided views in your RetailDW database are designed to make common analytics and reporting tasks much easier by pre-joining and aggregating data. Here are sample questions you can ask, leveraging each view for business insights:

---

1. dbo.vw_CustomerLifetimeValue

Purpose: Analyze customer value, loyalty, and segmentation.

Sample Questions:
Who are our top 10 customers by lifetime revenue?

What is the average order value for Gold tier customers?

Which region has the highest average customer tenure?

How many customers have placed more than 5 orders?

What is the distribution of customers by membership tier?


---

2. dbo.vw_MonthlySales

Purpose: Track sales performance over time.

Sample Questions:
What was the total revenue and profit for each month in 2023?

Which month had the highest profit margin in 2024?

How did order counts trend quarter-over-quarter last year?

What is the average discount given per month?

Which months had a negative profit margin?


---

3. dbo.vw_OrderSummary

Purpose: Analyze order details, customer-product relationships, and sales channels.

Sample Questions:
Show all orders placed by customer "Leslie Johnson" in 2024.

Which products were most frequently ordered in Q1 2023?

What is the average number of line items per order?

List all orders shipped to the "Northeast" region last month.

How many unique customers placed orders in March 2024?


---

4. dbo.vw_ProductPerformance

Purpose: Evaluate product sales, popularity, and customer feedback.

Sample Questions:
Which products had the highest total units sold in 2024?

What is the average rating for each product category?

List the top 5 brands by total revenue.

Which products have the highest return counts?

How many reviews did "ClearView Headphone QF19" receive?


---

General Tips for Using Views:

Use views for quick, high-level analytics without writing complex joins.

Filter by year, month, region, product, or customer for targeted insights.

Combine views with aggregations (SUM, AVG, COUNT) for summaries.

Use ORDER BY and TOP N to find best/worst performers.
